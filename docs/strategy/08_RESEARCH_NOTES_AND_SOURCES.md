# Research Notes and Sources

## How to use this file

This is the grounding layer for the work kit. The other documents reference source IDs like `[S1]`, `[S2]`, etc. This file explains what each source supported and why it matters.

## S1 — IAA / COMPLY industry snapshot
**Title:** 2025 Investment Adviser Industry Snapshot Shows Continued Growth in Demand for Adviser Services  
**Publisher:** Investment Adviser Association  
**Date:** May 29, 2025  
**URL:** https://www.investmentadviser.org/iaatoday/press-release/2025-investment-adviser-industry-snapshot-shows-continued-growth-in-demand-for-adviser-services/

**Why it matters:**  
Used for market size and small-firm profile. Key facts cited in the work kit include:
- 15,870 advisers in 2024
- 68.4 million clients
- $144.6 trillion AUM
- 92.7% of advisers had 100 or fewer employees
- 68.5% managed less than $1 billion
- 87.7% managed less than $5 billion

## S2 — SEC 2026 Examination Priorities
**Title:** Fiscal Year 2026 Report  
**Publisher:** U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission  
**Date:** 2025 (FY 2026 priorities release)  
**URL:** https://www.sec.gov/files/2026-exam-priorities.pdf

**Why it matters:**  
Used to support the “why now” regulatory case. Key takeaways used in the work kit:
- adviser fiduciary standards remain a priority
- exams focus on conflicts, costs, products, best execution
- compliance program effectiveness remains central
- marketing, valuation, trading, portfolio management, disclosure/filings, and custody are core exam areas
- never-examined and recently registered advisers are specifically called out

## S3 — SEC Form ADV / IARD FAQs
**Title:** Frequently Asked Questions on Form ADV and IARD  
**Publisher:** U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission  
**URL:** https://www.sec.gov/about/divisions-offices/division-investment-management/electronic-filing-investment-advisers-iard/frequently-asked-questions-form-adv-iard

**Why it matters:**  
Used to support deadline pain and workflow logic. Key items:
- annual updating amendments are due within 90 days after fiscal year end
- December 31 year-end means March 31 filing deadline
- SEC staff provides no general grace period
- firms are responsible for tracking and submitting timely filings
- IARD has limited reminder functionality if configured

## Key takeaways from the research

### Most important strategic conclusions
1. The RIA market is big, but more importantly it is heavily composed of small firms. [S1]
2. Independence / advisor movement remains active, which sustains a launch or transition wedge. [S9] [S10] [S11]
3. Regulatory obligations are active, current, and operationally burdensome. [S2] [S3] [S5] [S6] [S7] [S8]
4. Existing competitors are fragmented across support, compliance, and broader operations categories. [S12] [S13] [S14] [S15] [S16] [S17] [S18] [S19]
5. There is strong whitespace in a product that owns the journey from launch through ongoing examination-ready compliance.
6. AI is relevant, but only if it is implemented with transparency, review, and control. [S20]

### Best current wedge
**Launch + ongoing compliance cockpit for breakaway and emerging RIAs**

### Best expansion path
**Selected operational workflows after the product becomes the trusted system of record for compliance**
