# RIA Launch & Compliance OS — Working Strategy Kit

## What this is

This is a working kit for you and Codex, not a polished final deliverable. The goal is to give you a strong starting point for strategy, positioning, product shape, and MVP execution.

The central idea is simple: **do not start by building generic wealthtech**. Start by owning the most painful and intimidating moment in the lifecycle of a small or breakaway advisory firm: **going independent, becoming an RIA, and staying examination-ready from day one**.

That wedge is compelling because the problem is urgent, non-discretionary, expert-heavy, fragmented across tools and consultants, and structurally painful for small firms. If you win that wedge, there is a natural expansion path into a broader RIA operating system.

## Core thesis

The opportunity is not “another compliance calendar.”

The opportunity is an **RIA Launch & Compliance OS** that:

1. walks a founder or breakaway advisor through the process of becoming an RIA step by step;
2. translates legal and regulatory complexity into concrete actions, deadlines, and required artifacts;
3. becomes the ongoing compliance cockpit after launch;
4. builds evidence, documentation, and exam readiness into the workflow itself; and
5. later expands into adjacent operational workflows once the product has earned trust.

In other words: **from regulatory fog to operating clarity**.

## Why this can work

Several market dynamics make this a credible entry point:

- The RIA channel remains large and structurally attractive, and the industry is still dominated by small firms. [S1]
- Advisor movement and independence remain active themes, which means there is a persistent flow of people evaluating whether to launch or transition. [S9] [S10] [S11]
- SEC examination priorities for 2026 explicitly keep investment adviser fiduciary obligations, compliance programs, marketing, filings, and newly registered advisers in focus. [S2]
- Smaller firms are still digesting newer requirements like the Regulation S-P amendments, and the SEC itself held small-firm outreach ahead of the June 3, 2026 compliance date. [S5] [S6]
- Existing solutions are either service-heavy, compliance-point-solution-heavy, or broad ops platforms that do not clearly own the “become an RIA + stay compliant” journey. [S12] [S13] [S14] [S15] [S16] [S17] [S18] [S19]

## Product stance

The most important positioning decision is this:

**V1 is not a full wealth management platform.**  
It is not portfolio accounting, not custody, not a client portal, not performance reporting, and not “Salesforce for RIAs.”

**V1 is a launch + compliance cockpit with an exam-ready operating model.**

That focus is a feature, not a limitation.

## Practical V1 posture

The product should feel like:

- a control tower,
- a guided launch path,
- a compliance command center,
- an evidence and audit trail system,
- an “explain what to do next and why” layer.

It should not feel like:

- a generic task manager,
- a lawyer replacement,
- a broad but shallow “advisor super app,”
- a black-box AI engine making legal judgments.

## Strategic warning

There is a real temptation to build too much too early because the adjacent opportunities are obvious: CRM, client communication, performance snapshots, billing oversight, marketing workflows, vendor management, and private fund administration. Those are all real adjacencies.

But the best first move is still the same:

**Own the point where independence meets compliance complexity.**

## Notes on legal/compliance posture

This concept should be built around an explicit product boundary:

- the platform helps firms organize, understand, operationalize, document, and monitor obligations;
- the platform can generate drafts, checklists, recommendations, and risk flags;
- the platform should preserve human review, approvals, and audit trails;
- the platform should not present itself as legal advice.

That boundary is not a weakness. It is part of what makes the product trustworthy.

## Bottom line

If I were turning this into a real company thesis, it would read like this:

> We are building the operating system for becoming and running a small, modern, examination-ready RIA. We start with the moment of maximum pain—launch and compliance—and then expand into the broader workflows that determine whether an advisory firm can operate efficiently and scale with confidence.
